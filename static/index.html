Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Olin.link?

A: Olin.link is a URL shortener, much like bit.ly, is.gd, or tinyurl.com. It takes a long URL: 
https://docs.google.com/document/d/1WLh6RhVyW42e4iyw8OjJp_uu9bLQHZtzrr2XKscvndU/edit
and turns it into a short one: 
https://olin.link/VeryImportantDocument
As opposed to the long url, the short one is:
  • Shorter
  • Easier to remember
  • Easier to say out loud
  • Easier to print out and type back in (for example, if you put it on a poster)
  • Cooler

Q: Why should I use Olin.link instead of bit.ly/is.gd/tinyurl.com?

Because it's better! Those tools all have several limitations that Olin.link does not:
  • It's often hard to get a good shortlink, leaving you with something like bit.ly/OlinShopWorkOrder2. Because Olin.link is only used by the Olin community, these sorts of naming conflicts are much less likely.
  • When you do get a good shortlink (like tinyurl.com/menu), you're polluting the global address pool and preventing anybody else from using that shortlink ever again. With Olin.link, that's much less likely to be an issue because, for example, Olin only has one dining hall that only has one menu.
  • Olin.link shortlinks can be updated to point to a different long URL later on, even if the original owner has left the community—so olin.link/menu can live on forever as the link to the current dining hall menu, even if the full URL changes.
  • tinyurl.com/whatever is boring. Olin.link/whatever is cool, unique, and interesting.
  • By using other URL shortening services, you're exposing the community to privacy risks by forcing every request for a resource to go through a 3rd party with unknown data protection protocols. Olin.link is less risky because it's run by your fellow Oliners—who you hopefully trust a bit more than some random strangers on the internet—and because of our very strict privacy policy, which is listed below.

Q: What can I use Olin.link for? Are there any limitations? Does it cost money?

A: You're welcome to use olin.link for anything you'd like, and it's free! As always, please remember the Honor Code and to Be A Good Person.™
Caveat: Olin.link is provided on a best-effort basis. While it's fine to share a shortlink publicly, if it goes viral and Olin.link starts getting hit hundreds of times per second, it might stop working.

Q: Who's behind Olin.link?

Olin.link was built and is maintained by Ari Porad, a member of the Olin Class of 2024, because he got sick of stupid TinyURLs.

Q: Is Olin.link open-source? Can I contribute to it?

Yes! Olin.link's code can be found on GitHub, and contributions are welcome! Feel free to reach out to Ari for help if you're looking for a place to start.

Q: What's Olin.link's Privacy Policy?

Privacy is a fundemental human right, and Olin.link tries hard to respect your privacy.

When you create a shortlink, we collect and store:

  • The long URL that your shortlink points to
  • The short URL that you've requested, or a randomly-generated default
  • Your IP address, for fraud prevention
  • Your Olin email address, for fraud prevention, so we can reach out if there's an issue with your shortlink, and so we can know who's allowed to edit a shortlink.
  • The time

When somebody clicks on a shortlink, we collect and store:

  • The shortlink they requested
  • Their IP address, in anonymized form. Specifically, if they're on-campus or connecting from the Olin network, we store that. Otherwise, we store their IP address with the last octet zeroed out (for example, 12.34.56.78 is stored in anonymized form as 12.34.56.0).
  • The time

Here's how we use that information:

  • We use this information for the obvious purpose of matching shortlinks to long URLs
  • We use that information to allow you to change the long URL that your shortlink points to
  • We may use that information to combat fraud, improve the service, and generally understand how people are using Olin.link
  • We may use your email to contact you if we have concerns about one of your shortlinks or if there's another issue
  • We may provide any of this information to the Olin administration, StAR, the Honor Board, or another entity if we have a legitimite reason to believe that it's in the interests of the community
  • If we receive a valid legal order from a law enforcement authority with relevant jurisdiction, we'll be forced to turn over any of the data we've collected
